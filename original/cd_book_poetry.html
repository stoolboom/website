Growing up in Scotland, Chris was raised on a literary diet of the great Scottish poets such as the Bard of Ayrshire, Robert Burns, and the Tragedian of Dundee, William McGonagall. Keenly aware that the great 18th century Scottish poetic style has been falling out of favor since the 18th century, Chris has obtained his poetic license and and has begun work on his first poetic anthology.

To craft authentic 18th century prose, Chris employs only 18th century-sounding Scottish words, and is committed to writing about insignificant subjects such as small animals and obscure body parts.

With this in mind, he presents his first poetic work, Ode to an Ill Wind.


Ode to an Ill Wind (Original) Ode to an Ill Wind (Translation)
Och fousome, awfu', wratchit guff  O foulest, awful, wretched gas 
Tha spews aff oot lood frae ma duff  That issues loudly from my ass, 
O reeks mair vile nae man ca' blowst  Of smells more vile no man may boast 
Whit gied ye birth, th' coggle rowst?† What gave you birth, the crock pot roast? 
Whan willt ye lea' an blaw awiff?  When will you leave and waft away? 
Maun a for aye cast freens a'drif?  Must I fore'er keep friends at bay? 
A'll ett awthing a haud yer cowk  I'll try all things to ease your flow 
Whit maun a hae, a bouel bowk?  What must I have, a colon blow? 
†While there is no direct modern English equivalent to coggle rowst,
it is often rendered as meaty-pot, or crock roast.
